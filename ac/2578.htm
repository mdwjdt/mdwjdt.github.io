Potts(1905-1910) 2578

2578. And she was vindicated. That this signifies that thus there was no fault and no harm, is evident from all that precedes, of which this is a brief conclusion.

Elliott(1983-1999) 2578

2578. 'And she was vindicated' means that thus there was no blame attached and no harm done. This is clear from preceding statements to which this forms the conclusion.

Latin(1748-1756) 2578

2578. Quod `vindicata' significet quod sic nulla culpa et nulla noxa, constat ab omnibus illis quae praecedunt, quorum haec e: clausula.
