Spiritual Experiences (Odhner and Nemitz translation 1998) 2274

2274. When the spirits came forth, there appeared to me small clouds clumped together in different forms, some ending in rather sharp points, some in other ways, with variety. The clouds so clumped together were black, and it was then pointed out to me convincingly that they were that kind of spirits-I thought they were insane spirits-who were permitted to obsess me, for little clumps of cloud bunched together in various ways symbolize such spirits.

Spiritual Experiences (Buss translation 1902) 2274

2274. When the spirits came, then appeared to me little clouds aggregrated in various forms, part ending in sharp points [desinentibus acuminatim], part otherwise, with variety: the clouds thus aggregrated were black, and it was then indicated to me by persuasion that they were such spirits: I thought that they were insane spirits, who were permitted to obsess me, for such were signified by the clumps of cloud, aggregated together variously.

Experientiae Spirituales 2274 (original Latin 1748-1764)

2274. Cum veniebant spiritus, tunc apparebant mihi nubeculae conglomeratae variis formis, partim desinentibus acuminatius, partim aliter, cum varietate, nubes ita conglomeratae erant nigrae, et mihi tunc indicabatur per persuasionem, quod essent tales spiritus-putabam 1

quod essent spiritus insani-quibus 2

permissum esset me obsidere, tales enim nubis glomuli simul conglomerati varie, significabant.

Footnotes:

1. The Manuscript has spiritus, putabam

2. The Manuscript has insani, quibus
