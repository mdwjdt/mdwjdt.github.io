Spiritual Experiences (Odhner and Nemitz translation 1998) 2344

2344. This also shows that those who are in a restful state, that is, whom the Lord holds and keeps in a restful state, cannot be effected at all by evil spirits, in all their efforts, and that they should flee away from them entirely, for they perceive the restless gang as a turbulent and raging sea, which they flee.

Spiritual Experiences (Buss translation 1902) 2344

2344. Hence it may also be manifest that there are those who are in a quiet state, that is, whom the Lord keeps and preserves in a quiet state, and that evil spirits can effect nothing with all their endeavors, and that they must needs fly thence precipitately [prorsus], for they perceive the restless crowd, like a troublous and tumultuous sea, which they flee.

Experientiae Spirituales 2344 (original Latin 1748-1764)

2344. Exinde quoque constare potest, quod ii qui in statu quieto sunt, hoc est, quos Dominus in statu quietis tenet et conservat, quod mali spiritus [eos] nihil, omnibus suis conatibus, efficere possint, et quod prorsus inde aufugere debeant, nam percipiunt turbam irrequietam, sicut turbulentum et tumultuosum mare, quod fugiunt.
