Spiritual Experiences (Odhner and Nemitz translation 1998) 2326

2326. That still, the Lord is not even in the least respect the cause of evil, follows from the fact that when the Life of the Lord streams into the heavens, and into the world of spirits, then organic forms or substances receive the life, each according to its own form, some in this way, and some in that, thus with unlimited variety. The Life streaming in they imagine to be their own, and due to this, perversities arise. 1748, 15 June.

Spiritual Experiences (Buss translation 1902) 2326

2326. That yet the Lord is nowise not in the least the cause of evil, follows from this, that when the life of the Lord flows into the heavens and the world of spirits, then the organic forms or substances receive life, each one according to its form, some in this way, some in that, thus with indefinite variety. The life which inflows is supposed by them to be their own, hence [arise] perversities. - 1748, June 15.

Experientiae Spirituales 2326 (original Latin 1748-1764)

2326. Quod usque Dominus in nulla, et ne in minima quidem causa mali, consequitur [ex eo] 1

, quod Vita Domini cum influit in coelos, et in mundum spirituum, tunc formae seu substantiae organicae, recipiunt vitam, quaelibet secundum suam formam, quidam ita, et [quidam] 2

aliter, sic cum indefinita varietate; Vita quae influit, putatur ab iis sua esse, inde perversitates. 1748, 15 Junius.
