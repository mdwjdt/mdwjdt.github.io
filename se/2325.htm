Spiritual Experiences (Odhner and Nemitz translation 1998) 2325

2325. No Person on earth and no spirit, nor any angel thinks, wills, does anything on his or her own power, and to think they do is a fallacy of the senses, and fantasy; and still, the Lord is not the cause of evil

This was put to spirits who imagine they think, will and do everything on their own power, and they thought the proposition was absurd, because as they perceive it, they think, will and act on their own power. But still it is the truth, and it follows from it, that they are mere instrumental substances, and the Lord Alone is life.

Spiritual Experiences (Buss translation 1902) 2325

2325. THAT NEITHER MAN, SPIRITS, NOR ANGELS THINK, WILL, OR ACT FROM THEMSELVES, AND THEIR THINKING SO IS A FALLACY OF SENSE, AND A PHANTASY; AND YET, THAT THE LORD IS NOT THE CAUSE OF EVIL.

This proposition was laid before spirits who suppose that they think, will, and do everything from themselves, and they supposed that this was absurd, because they seem to themselves to perceive that they think, will, and act from themselves; but yet it is the truth, and the consequence thereof is, that they are mere organic substances, and the Lord alone is life.

Experientiae Spirituales 2325 (original Latin 1748-1764)

2325. Quod Homo et spiritus, nec angelus quicquam ex se cogitet, velit, agat, et quod id cogitent, sit fallacia 1

sensus, et phantasia; et tamen quod Dominus non est in causa mali

Hoc propositum est spiritibus, qui putant ex se cogitare, omnia velle et agere, et putabant, quod hoc phantasticum esset, quia percipere sibi videntur, quod ex se cogitent, velint et agant: sed usque est veritas, et consequens ejus, quod sint merae substantiae organicae, et Solus Dominus vita.
