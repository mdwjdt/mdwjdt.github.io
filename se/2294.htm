Spiritual Experiences (Odhner and Nemitz translation 1998) 2294

2294. This is also the reason why spirits' states of mind vary, so that they are comparable to the changes of the year, and of the day, morning, noon, evening, night, then again morning, or spring, summer, fall, winter, then again spring, but with countless differences. For the angels the states vary only as the noon, evening, night and morning 1in the springtime, and so on, 1748, 11 June. So I have noticed that what was happy and cheerful to certain spirits, for the angels was cheerless and almost unhappy; because all things are relative.

Footnotes:

1. The original has the word ver, "spring."

Spiritual Experiences (Buss translation 1902) 2294

2294. Which also is the reason that the states of spirits are varied, so that thereto may be compared the vicissitudes of the year, as also of the day, to wit, morning, noon, evening, and night, also morning, or spring, summer, autumn, winter, also spring. But with indefinite difference; with the angels [the changes] are like its noon, evening, and night, only as in springtime, and they have spring, and so forth. - 1748, June 11. Wherefore I observed that what constituted the felicity and gladness of some spirits was to the angels cheerless and unhappy; because all things are relative.
